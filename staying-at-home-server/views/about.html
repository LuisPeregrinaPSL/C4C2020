Staying@Home Team

We are an open source project designed to build solutions focused on making people stay at home more straightforward, using gamification, our mobile app was designed to help people to get some fun during lockdown, while contributing to reduce risk of virus spread.

The Challenge

The most effective action to prevent the virus spread on health emergencies is the lockdown and/or stay at home policies.

However, countries are facing big challenges to make sure people meets the policies. As a result, there are multiple actions implemented by governments to invite/force people to stay at home, these actions are being applied to broad areas such as cities, states, countries:

  • Security checkpoints on streets
  • Policy patrols asking people to go home
  • Multiple advertising and sensitization campaigns
  • Sanitizer tunnels in crowded places

Our Solution

Staying@Home App: tracks how long the users have stayed at home and sends the statistics to the data analyzer.

By using gamification and VR, the app allows the user to build a forest at their virtual home and contribute to their community:

  • For each 6 consecutive hours at home, user gets a tree
  • Every 6 trees, the user is move to next level
  • If user leaves home before the 6 hours needed to get a tree, the one in progress is deleted, and then a new one starts growing from 0 once user gets back home.
  • As more trees are added to the forest, user can get additional items and go through different contribution levels
  • There is a GPS tracking component monitoring if the users moves enough to consider that they leave home
  • User can share on social networks their progress and achievements.

Staying@Home Dashboard: Online platform receiving data and statistics about the time that people stays at home. The platform uses kibana and elastic search to build real time graphs showing which areas better meet the stay at home restrictions.

 

Together we can help our public health systems and government to optimize resources
